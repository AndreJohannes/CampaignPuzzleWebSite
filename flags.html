PSYDEH Donor Puzzle

WHY A DONOR PUZZLE?

Here at “Fruits of Change” campaign central, we celebrate you, the global citizen donor, AND progress towards our $25,000 goal – money PSYDEH and indigenous women in Mexico will use in these specific ways to drive sustainable development of self-reliant communities from the bottom-up.

How do we use the puzzle to highlight campaign progress?
Each of the 2500 puzzle pieces represents a $10usd donation. So, say you donate the equivalent of $100usd, we add 10 more pieces. Boom!

How does the puzzle celebrate you?
Each puzzle piece is connected to an individual donor! Scroll over the image to find out who donated, how much and from where. One click on the “Global Donors’ Flags” button in the top left corner reveals a cool visual representation of global citizens’ strength when united. Double boom!

WHAT IS THE IMAGE?

It’s an embroidered map of Tenango de Doria, the heart of the Otomí-Tepehua region in which PSYDEH and its native women partners live and work.

Why an embroidered map?
For centuries, the Otomí people of Tenango de Doria have practiced a globally famous style of embroidery involving figures called “Tenangos".

Why the “Fruits of Change” campaign and Tenangos?
Communal self-reliance depends upon economic stability. PSYDEH’s multi-year program links community organizing and education work with honest economic gain. It supports native women’s desire to build a regional cooperativeto globally sell artisanal goods like this embroidered map. Our campaign invests in native women producers in two ways. First, your donation pays for this specific work by PSYDEH and native women partners. Second, all donors making a $500 and $1000usd donation will receive embroidered gifts purchased from women artisan partners. Yeah! A win-win-win!
